THE ELECTRONICS ENGINEERING AND TECHNICIANS LICENSURE EXAMINATIONS

The Department of Electronics Engineering of Rizal Technological University, together with the Electronics Engineering and Technology Students’ Society (ECETSS), extends its warmest congratulations to the successful passers of the Electronics Engineer Licensure Examination (ECELE) and the Electronics Technician Licensure Examination (ECTLE).

This milestone is a reflection not only of the hard work, perseverance, and dedication of our graduates, but also of the department’s unwavering commitment to excellence in engineering education. Through rigorous training, innovative instruction, and holistic development, RTU continues to produce graduates who are ready to meet the challenges of the profession and contribute to nation-building.

Performance Results:
Electronics Engineering (ECE) — 50.00% (National Passing: 45.39%)
Electronics Technician (ECT) — 65.63% (National Passing: 81.75%)

The College of Engineering takes pride in these achievements, as they highlight the strength of RTU’s academic foundation and the resilience of its students. Our new Electronics Engineers and Electronics Technicians now carry the responsibility of applying their knowledge and skills to create solutions, foster innovation, and uphold the values of professionalism and integrity in the field of electronics.

Elective Courses in Electronics Engineering

The Electronics Engineering (ECE) curriculum at RTU offers a wide range of specialized electives designed to equip students with advanced technical expertise and prepare them for diverse career paths. Each elective track provides focused training in key areas of modern electronics and technology.

Course A: Telecommunication

Advanced Communication Systems & Design (Wireless)

-This elective provides in-depth knowledge of wireless communication technologies. Students will learn to design, analyze, and optimize modern communication systems that support today’s fast-paced digital world.

Advanced Networking

-A course centered on the backbone of global connectivity, enhancing students’ expertise in network design, management, and security to address the growing demand for reliable and scalable infrastructures.

Course B: Power Electronics

Advanced Power Supply Systems

-Focuses on the design, development, and application of efficient power supply systems, with emphasis on their role in industrial and consumer electronic devices.

Renewable Energy Systems

-Introduces students to sustainable energy technologies and innovations. This elective highlights renewable energy solutions, empowering students to contribute to the transition toward greener and more efficient power systems.

Course C: Instrumentation and Control

Advanced Instrumentation & Control Systems

-Strengthens competencies in measurement, monitoring, and industrial automation, with an emphasis on precision and innovation in controlling dynamic processes.

Robotics Technology

-Provides hands-on training in robotics and intelligent systems, preparing students to integrate automation into various applications and drive advancements in smart industry technologies.

Course D: Building Information & Communications Technology Infrastructure

ICT Infrastructure

-Focuses on the design and implementation of robust ICT frameworks that support smart cities and enterprise-level systems.

Electronics Ancillary Systems

-Covers the integration of auxiliary electronic systems such as security, communication, and automation, equipping students with practical skills in modern building technologies.
